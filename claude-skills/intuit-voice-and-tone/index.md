# Intuit Content Design — Reference Index

This index describes the content categories available on [contentdesign.intuit.com](https://contentdesign.intuit.com). Use it to identify which section covers a given content design question, then consult the live site for full guidance.

---

## Foundations
Core content skills required of all Intuit content practitioners.

- **About this site** — Purpose and scope of the Content Design System; AP Stylebook as default style authority; when context may justify departing from guidance.
- **The basics** — Twelve rules for writing better Intuit content: correct product name capitalization, mobile-first writing, sentence case, contractions, benefits-first ordering, localization, and peer review.
- **Writing small** — Conciseness principles: structuring for scanners, progressive disclosure, content hierarchy, character-reduction techniques (filler words, active voice, sentence fragments, hard-working verbs).
- **Emotional design** — Writing that builds emotional relationships: avoiding hype language, using tone that respects customer state, avoiding manipulative patterns like confirm-shaming.
- **Content testing** — Practical guide to test methods: cloze, comprehension, card sorting, A/B, customer interviews, and codesign. Covers sample sizes, analysis, and cognitive biases.
- **Content strategy 101** — Seven-topic self-paced course: content inventories, audits, gap analysis, test plans, strategic stakeholder partnering, systems thinking, and content architecture.
- **How we work** — Cross-functional collaboration guidance; argument for including content designers, visual designers, researchers, PMs, and developers from the first customer interview.
- **Tools and apps** — Reference for tools used by Intuit content designers: AP Stylebook, Merriam-Webster, Writer (AI grammar checking), Figma, Mural, FigJam, Google Drive.

## Style & Usage
Editorial conventions across six topic areas.

- **Our principles** — Foundational writing rules: conversational over formal grammar, second/first/third person usage, active voice, approved contractions, simple verb tenses, 20-word sentence limit for localization, global writing considerations.
- **Formatting** — Capitalization (sentence case, exceptions for brands), bold (UI elements only), italics avoidance, lists, links, tables, URLs, address formats for six locales, acronyms, and abbreviations.
- **Numbers** — Numerals vs. words, currency formats for six locales, date formats, phone number formats for seven locales, time (12-hour vs. 24-hour by region), sensitive data masking.
- **Punctuation** — Mark-by-mark rules: ampersands, apostrophes, asterisks, brackets, colons, serial Oxford comma, dashes (em, en, hyphen), ellipses, exclamation points, hashtags, parentheses, percent, periods, pipes, question marks, quotation marks, semicolons, slashes.
- **Product names** — Correct spelling and capitalization for Credit Karma, ProConnect, QuickBooks Online, TurboTax, Mailchimp; rules for multiple product versions; QuickBooks Desktop vs. Online migration content.
- **Emoji** — When and how to use emoji: single emoji per message, placement at sentence end, default to yellow skin tone, screen reader alt text behavior, cross-platform rendering, prohibited contexts (bad news, puns).

## Product & UI
Content guidelines for the primary interface touchpoints in Intuit products.

- **Product content design** — Philosophy: audience segmentation, task focus, reducing cognitive load, evidence-based testing, accessible UI considerations, conciseness principles.
- **Actions (CTAs)** — Buttons and links: 24-character max for buttons, 6–8 words for links, verb-first construction, sentence case, no emoji/punctuation, device-agnostic language.
- **Confirmations** — Dialog design: three triggers (irreversible actions, data loss, unexpected shared-state changes); title/body/CTA structure; avoiding yes/no or double-negative pairings.
- **Empty states** — Patterns for first-time use, absent/deleted data, and task completion; four writing principles (clear, helpful, brief, inspiring action); three-part structure (headline, body, CTA).
- **Errors** — Four writing principles (clarity, brevity, helpfulness, specificity); severity framework (minor/moderate/severe); words to avoid (invalid, whoops, oops, sorry); three-part structure; field-level vs. page-level placement.
- **Fields** — Form field conventions: labels (short, outside field), placeholder text (supplemental only), required-field indicators (asterisk or text), field-level error language, ARIA accessibility.
- **Notifications** — Three delivery types (in-product, push, text); three urgency levels; character-count optimization by device; CTA clarity; frequency limits; iOS and Android conventions.
- **Mobile** — Mobile-first strategy: seven ways mobile differs from desktop; 12-point content checklist; tap vs. select terminology; native app vs. responsive web conventions.
- **Tooltips** — Trigger conditions, content limits (3-line max, 60-char header, 130-char body), when tooltips are unnecessary, single tooltips vs. guided tours (3–4 ordered tooltips), accessibility.
- **Writing about UI** — Referencing interface elements: capitalization rules, action verbs (select/choose/tap/go to), bold usage, icons, dropdown menus, navigation, directional cues.

## Talking to Customers
Context-specific guidelines for high-stakes customer communication.

- **Bad news** — Empathetic delivery of difficult messages: leading with bad news upfront, focusing on solutions, staying transparent, when and how to apologize, avoiding judgmental language.
- **Celebrations** — When and how to celebrate accomplishments: three-level framework (recognition moments, milestones, major achievements); avoiding tone-deaf scenarios; accessibility for celebratory visuals.
- **Consent & permission** — Designing consent experiences: when consent is required, what it must communicate, concise positive copy, preferred CTAs (Agree/I agree), patterns to avoid, legal review requirements.
- **Developers** — Documentation for developer audiences: four attributes (clear, concise, accurate, structured); writing tips and examples for each; how headlines serve navigation and findability.
- **Emails** — Subject lines (character counts, personalization, spam avoidance), preheaders, salutations, body copy, layout, CTAs, footer legal requirements, success metrics (open rate, CTR, conversion).
- **Experts** — Communicating with CPAs, enrolled agents, ProAdvisors, and support agents: five principles (respect expertise, act as partner, say just enough, speak to purpose, illustrate impact).
- **Help** — In-line help types (tooltips, guided tours, point-of-need links) and help articles (how-tos, concept overviews, FAQs, troubleshooting); article anatomy; four editorial goals.
- **Money terms** — Two approaches for financial/tax terminology: state term first for familiar terms; describe concept first for unfamiliar accounting terms. Both empower customers with professional vocabulary.
- **Security & data** — When to discuss security, benefit-focused framing over fear tactics, avoiding exploitable implementation details, privacy vs. security distinction, jargon guidance, legal partnership.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Five areas of accessible and inclusive content design.

- **Accessible content** — POUR framework (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust): screen-reader writing, color contrast, captions, form accessibility, plain language, platform-agnostic robustness.
- **Alt text** — Required for images, icons, and visual media; concise, purposeful, under 125 characters; starting with a capital letter; rules for decorative images, captions as an alternative.
- **Anti-racist language** — Nine principles; self-assessment framework; word list of terms to retire (whitelist, blacklist, master/slave, tribe, grandfathered) with approved substitutions.
- **Inclusive content** — People-first language, gender neutrality, cultural diversity; Eurocentric defaults; collecting only necessary personal data; gender-neutral pronouns and greetings; Romance-language localization.
- **Readability** — Target of 5th–8th grade: shorter sentences and words, familiar vocabulary, simple verb tenses, readability scoring tools (Flesch-Kincaid, Gunning Fog, Writer app).

## AI
Content guidelines for AI experiences across five areas.

- **Foundations** — Four core AI principles (foster trust with transparency, focus on customer benefit, reduce cognitive load, be brief and clear); conversation design via Gricean cooperative maxims plus five additional maxims.
- **Voice & tone for AI** — Guide persona with two attributes: plainspoken (straightforward, conversational) and genuine (sincere, candid, transparent); tone defaults to neutral and professional; Mailchimp levity exception noted.
- **Using AI** — Decision criteria for generated vs. dynamic vs. static content; prompt engineering (few-shot examples, contextual background, keyword emphasis, negative constraints); universal voice prompt; evaluation rubric; NotebookLM for scaling workflows.
- **UX patterns** — Trust and transparency patterns (disclaimers, AI review notices, status indicators, planning-doing-done structure); conversation flow patterns (error handling, disambiguation, escalation, fallback, welcome messages).
- **Writing about AI** — How to refer to AI in product copy: focus on customer outcomes, not technology; terminology (agentic, Powered by AI, done for you, Intuit Intelligence vs. Intuit AI); capitalization and bold usage rules.
