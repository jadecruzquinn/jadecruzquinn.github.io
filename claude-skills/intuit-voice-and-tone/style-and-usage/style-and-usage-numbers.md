# Numbers

**Source:** https://contentdesign.intuit.com/style-and-usage/numbers/

---

## Get it right when it counts

We usually use numerals (1, 2, 3, and so forth) instead of spelling out the number.

When representing specific statistics or examples, use numerals for all numbers up to a billion even if at the beginning of the line or sentence.

Spell out casual expressions (You're one in a million).

Spell out ordinal numbers (first) up to and including ninth when indicating sequence or location. Use numerals starting with 10th (He was second in the match, but 12th in the tournament.).

### Use numerals for

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Age for people and animals (but not things) | An 8-year-old girl, a five-year-old tax law |
| Decades | the 1980s, '80s |
| Ratios | ratio of 2 to 1 |
| Groups | 1 of 10 groups |
| Percentages | 12%, 0.4% |

### Examples

| Do | Notes |
|----|-------|
| $25K, $20B, $500M | Use numerals for statistics |
| 9th U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals | Numerals from 10th onward |
| First, enter your email address. | Spell out ordinal numbers 1st–9th |
| That one photo generated thousands of comments. | Spell out casual expressions |
| We gathered insights from 6,058 individuals in 20 midmarket cities in the UK. | Use numerals for specific data |

Different languages format numbers differently. Work with regional content designers to make sure we format numbers appropriately.

## Abbreviating the word "number"

The preferred abbreviation for number is **no.** with a period.

Don't use **#** in place of the word number unless there are significant space restrictions, such as in a mobile screen.

Check with regional content designers for acceptable abbreviations in specific countries. For example, n° is the accepted abbreviation in France.

## Bold numbers

You can use bold to highlight UI elements such as numbers in instructional text. Don't use it just for emphasis.

Use only one treatment at a time (color, bold, italics, indentation).

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Enter this validation code: **JFI129-XMW8R-32U47**. | Don't use bold just for visual emphasis |
| In the Search field, enter **lmnop.qrs**. | Don't use multiple treatments at once |

## Currency

For negative amounts, place the minus symbol in front of the currency symbol.

You can specify which country by adding the currency code after the amount if needed.

### Australia

| Example | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| $25 | Most common |
| AUD$25.00 | Only when relevant (like comparing overseas prices) |
| -$25 | Negative amounts |
| $25.00 | With cents |


### Brazil

Leave a space between the currency symbol and the amount. Use a comma to separate reais and cents.

- R$ 1.234,50
- R$ 25 / R$ 25,00

### Canada and US

Don't add a space between the currency symbol and the amount ($10.20, not $ 10.20).

If the amount is less than one dollar, use the cents symbol (25¢) for accessibility and clarity. In product entry fields, use 0.25 (no cents symbol).

Include commas in amounts in the thousands and greater.

| Example | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| $25.00 USD | With currency code |
| $25.00 CAD | International context |
| $5,000 | Use commas in thousands |
| 50¢ | Amounts under one dollar |
| -$25 | Negative amounts |

### France

Don't add a space between the currency symbol and the amount (€10, not € 10).

- €25 000.50
- €25.50
- 25 euros
- €25

### UK

Don't add a space between the currency symbol and the amount (£10, not £ 10).

- £100,000.00 (or just £100,000)
- £3.50
- 50p

## Dates

For placeholder text in form fields, use lowercase letters (ex: mm/dd/yyyy).

Don't use ordinal numbers (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.) in dates.

For month and year only, use Month yyyy. If shortening for fiscal years, use FY with the last two digits and no spaces (FY25, FY26). This is jargon, so make sure it's right for the audience.

Spell out days of the week for specific dates. Only abbreviate months in 3 letters (Mar, Aug, Oct) for UI elements like tables, links, and footers.

In extreme space-constrained cases, 1-letter abbreviations are OK. Mobile uses 3-letter month abbreviations (Mar 9).

For countries using 12-hour time, capitalize AM and PM with no periods.

To indicate a decade, add an "s" without an apostrophe (1970s, 1990s).

### Australia

Use dd/mm/yyyy (without a leading zero) or dd Month, yyyy.

| Example |
|---------|
| The site will be down for maintenance on Sunday, 18 August at 9 PM AWT. |
| 15 Mar |
| 30/11/2018 |
| 31 January, 2007 |

### Brazil

Use dd/mm/yyyy (without a leading zero) or dd month, yyyy. Months are always lowercase.

| Example |
|---------|
| The site will be down for maintenance on Sunday, 18 august at 9h. |
| 15 mar |
| 30/11/2018 |
| 31 january, 2007 |

### Canada

Use dd/mm/yyyy (without a leading zero) or Month dd, yyyy.

| Example |
|---------|
| The site will be down for maintenance on Sunday, August 18 at 9 PM PT. |
| M-F, 7 AM to 9 PM PT |
| Mar 15 |
| Saturday, December 8 |
| November 2016 |
| December 31, 2016 |
| 31/12/2016 |

### France

Use dd/mm/yyyy (without a leading zero) or dd Month, yyyy.

| Example |
|---------|
| The site will be down for maintenance on Sunday, 18 August at 9 h. |
| 15 Mar |
| November 2016 |
| 14 November 2016 |
| Wednesday, 3 November 2016 |
| 15/06/2016 |

### UK

Use dd/mm/yyyy, dd/mm, or dd Month, yyyy.

| Example |
|---------|
| The site will be down for maintenance on Sunday, 18 August at 9 PM. |
| 31 Aug |
| 31/08 |
| 31/08/2016 |

### US

Use mm/dd/yyyy (without a leading zero) or Month dd, yyyy.

For ranges within the same month: Sep 18–24. For ranges spanning 2 months: Aug 27–Sep 1.

| Example |
|---------|
| The site will be down for maintenance on Sunday, August 18 at 9 PM PT. |
| M-F, 7 AM to 9 PM PT |
| Sep 1-15 |
| Mar 15 |
| Saturday, December 8 |
| November 2016 |
| November 11, 2016 |
| 11/11/2016 |
| 3/9/2018 |

## Decimals

Carry decimals to the hundredth place (33.76). Omit zeroes when possible unless the number is a dollar amount.

Use decimals (0.75) instead of fractions.

---

## Footnotes

Try not to use footnotes. Asterisks and footnotes slow readers down—you're interrupting their train of thought and asking them to hunt for secondary information.

If you need to use footnotes, insert a number in superscript at the end of the sentence. Corresponding footnotes live at the bottom of the page or screen.

If citing legal copy with more than one citation, use footnotes. Don't put the footnote in the middle of the sentence. Work with your legal partner.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Put footnotes at the end of a sentence: "The #1 iPad POS—QuickBooks Point of Sale.¹ Clear business data anytime.²" | Don't put footnotes in the middle of a sentence: "The #1 iPad POS¹—QuickBooks Point of Sale." |
| One footnote per sentence | Don't put multiple footnotes in one sentence |

## ID numbers and sensitive data

To protect users' privacy, we mask some personal information. A character is masked after a 2-second delay or immediately after another character is entered. Use black dots (●)—also called midline ellipses—to mask data (Option+8 on a Mac).

- Use one dot to mask each character in sensitive data patterns.
- Mask letter strings with 4 black dots, truncating no more than 4.
- Mask all account numbers except the last four with four black dots.

Check with your product information security team if you’re not sure what to mask.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| We responded to your email at ca●●●@gmail.com | ca***@gmail.com |
| You gave us phone number ●●●-●●●-8437 | x-1234 |
| Your Social Insurance number: ●●● ●●● 123 | ● 1234 |
| Your Social Security number: ●●●-●●-1234 | ****1234 |
| Your date of birth (12/12/●●●●) | ●●/●●/●●●● |
| You paid from account ●●●●1234 | xxxx-1234 |

## Numbers with commas

Use commas in numbers with 4 or more digits ($5,000).

Don't use commas in error numbers, page numbers, or addresses.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Your profit rose 4,500% | 4500% |
| 1,234.56 in English = 1 234,56 in French | Don't ignore regional formatting |

## Phone numbers

Phone numbers are formatted differently depending on the country. For all locales: include the area code, leave off the country code, and don't bold anything. For any not listed, follow the international standards for formatting (ITU-T E.164).

### Australia

Use spaces. Leave off the +61 before the STD code.

- 0422 123 456 (mobile)
- 1234 5678 ext. 753
- 04 1234 5678

### Brazil

Use dashes in most phone numbers and a space to separate the area code. Use ramal for extension.

- 0800 047 4094
- 3456-6070 ramal 234
- 11 99999-5060

### Canada

Use dashes. Leave out the 1 before the area code except for toll free numbers.

- 1-888-829-8589 (toll free)
- 123-456-7890 ext. 234
- 123-456-7890

### France

Use spaces between sets of 2 numbers. Leave out the +33.

- 0805 220 189 (toll free)
- 06 24 55 32 14

### India

Landline numbers are 8 digits, split into groups of 4 digits with 2- or 3-digit city codes. Mobile: add country code (+91), a space, then the 10-digit number.

- +91 9978902123
- 080 2245 7890

### UK

Use spaces. Leave out the +44.

- 7986 123 456 (mobile)
- 0300 123 4567 (for companies)
- 07986 123 456

### US

Use dashes. Leave out the 1 before the area code.

- 800-558-9558 ext. 1234
- 888-777-1234

## Time

When specifying length of a video, use the 0:00 format:

- 1:40:12
- 10:39
- 3:26
- 0:30

When talking about time zones outside the target audience's region, use 24-hour time with UTC offsets:

- New York: UTC-5
- Singapore: UTC+6

It's OK to shorten intervals of time (years, months, quarters) to save space:

| Abbreviation | Full form |
|-------------|-----------|
| $20/mo | $20 per month |
| Next qtr | Next quarter |
| This yr | This year |

### Australia, Canada, and US

Express time as H:MM AM/PM time zone. Include a space before AM or PM, but no periods.

It's OK to drop minutes if it's on the hour. Noon and midnight can be referred to as just that, or as 12 AM and 12 PM.

| Time zone | Abbreviation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Australian Western Time | AWT |
| Australian Central Time | ACT |
| Australian Eastern Time | AET |
| Pacific Time | PT |
| Mountain Time | MT |
| Central Time | CT |
| Eastern Time | ET |

Use abbreviations (PT, ET) when combined with a time reference (5 AM PT). Spell out Eastern Time, Pacific Time when standing alone.

Note: We no longer refer to Standard/Daylight Time.

**Examples**: midnight, noon, 7-9 PM, 3 to 4:30 PM AWT, 11:45 PM ET, 8 AM PT

### Brazil

Use 24-hour time without time zones. Don't put a space between the number and the h.

- Meia-noite (midnight)
- Meio-dia (noon)
- 17:25 (only in tables, technical use, departure boards)
- 17h25
- 10h

### France

Use 24-hour time without time zones. Leave a space between the number and the h.

- 13:15 (tables, technical use)
- 9 h
- 13 h 15

### UK

Use 12-hour times with periods instead of colons.

- 1.45 PM

## Units of measure

Leave a space between the number and the unit of measure and use open punctuation (no periods). Use a hyphen if using the number with the unit of measure as an adjective.

For Australia, Brazil, Canada, France, and UK, use the metric system.

For the U.S., use the imperial system and provide metric equivalents in parentheses where useful.

| Do | Type |
|----|------|
| 8 fl oz, 25 lbs | Imperial (US) |
| 25 kg, 10 cm | Metric |
| 35 mph | Imperial speed |
| 50 km/h | Metric speed |
| one inch (2.54 cm) | Imperial with metric equivalent |
| 2-day workshop | Hyphenated adjective |
| 3.4-GHz processor | Hyphenated adjective |
| on hold for 3 minutes | No hyphen (no noun following) |
| 128 MB of RAM | Space between number and unit |
| 10 mL | Space between number and unit |
