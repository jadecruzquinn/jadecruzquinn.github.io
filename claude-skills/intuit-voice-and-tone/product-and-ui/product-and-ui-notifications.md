# Notifications

**Source:** https://contentdesign.intuit.com/product-and-ui/notifications/

---

## Types of notifications

We use several types of notifications to communicate with customers on their devices and in their Intuit products.

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **In-product notifications** | Messages that appear in the customer's product, next to the related action. Provide guidance and information. Usually stored in a notification center. |
| **Push notifications** | Sent from an app to the customer's device. Tell the customer to open the app for a specific reason. Only on-screen for a few seconds—easy to miss. |
| **Text messages** | Customers get text or notifications on their messaging app. The app doesn't have to be installed, but customers must opt-in. Messages often have links and are available until deleted. |

## Voice and tone

The voice and tone of notifications should be based on what the customer needs. Functional language is often better than emotional language, but think about the customer's last interaction and what they could be doing.

| Situation | Tone |
|-----------|------|
| Alerts and urgent to-do's | More functional |
| Milestones and celebrations | More emotional |

**Example**: A notification about taxes should be clear and concise. A notification about being paid is a time to celebrate.

### Urgency levels

There are 3 levels of urgency that affect voice and tone.

#### Low urgency

These messages give customers information based on an action they took in the product. They can be about an app-specific improvement, like a new feature.

> (Mileage tracking)
> Turn it on. Take a trip. Identify it as a business expense.

#### Medium urgency

These help the customer in a clear way. They might encourage customers, help them celebrate, identify opportunities, or get through a workflow. The benefit is front and center.

> **Great news!** Your federal tax return was accepted by the IRS.

#### High urgency

High-attention, time-sensitive messages to resolve or complete an action. We take customers to the exact point in the product to get the job done.

> **Share your docs ASAP**
> Your expert needs them by April 7 to file on time. After that, you can do your own taxes with Live Expert help instead.

## General writing tips

### Consider timing

Notifications interrupt people, so only send them when it's important and you have a good reason. If sent at a bad time, the person might miss it—or turn off notifications or delete the app.

- Avoid sending notifications at night or too early in the morning (based on the person's time zone). The only exception is urgent updates.
- Less is more—don't bombard customers with notifications.

### Optimize for readability

Devices and operating systems have different character count requirements for titles and descriptions. Instead of focusing on the max character count, think about [limits by device](https://clevertap.com/blog/what-are-push-notification-character-limits/) and [optimizing for readability](https://baymard.com/blog/line-length-readability).

### Keep it short and to the point

Notifications should be clear and concise.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| You can submit tax returns electronically beginning Thursday, January 28, 2021. [Get more details] | The IRS announced on Jan 15, 2021 that the official federal e-File start date for tax year 2020 will be Feb 12, 2021. In order to allow our tax professionals to serve their clients without workflow disruption… [out of characters] |
| Your 2021 federal return was accepted! | Did you know we have QuickBooks Insurance? It's really easy to set up if you're eligible, and it'll change your life! Just go to Setting then search an— [out of characters] |
| The IRS has extended the deadline to May 15. | We're pleased to announce that the deadline to file your taxes (previously April 15) has been extended by the IRS for another month. |

### Provide a clear action

The call to action (CTA) should set the expectation of what happens next—whether that's getting something done, learning something valuable, or fixing something quickly.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| We're making improvements to Square deposits. Take a minute to confirm the bank account you use. [Confirm account] | We've got news for you! [Get the deets] |
| Your credit card will expire in 30 days. [Update your info now] | The card you have on file is going to expire. |
| You have 2 invoices overdue by 13 days. [Set up auto reminders] | Overdue invoices need your attention. |

### Make it count

Don't announce every little thing. Give customers the right information at the right time.

Before writing a notification, ask yourself:

- Will this disrupt their workflow?
- What action are we asking them to take?
- Who's the target audience of this message?
- What's the customer benefit of this message?
- Is this the best channel for this message?

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| You can now track and file your sales tax through QuickBooks. [Find out how] | Time Tracking is here, save 50% |
| Collect and organize your client documents directly from ProConnect. [Create a request] | Turn on pay-enabled invoices to save time |
| Schedule a live video appointment with your bookkeeper. [Schedule appointment] | Time to get your books in order. Schedule an appointment with our tax professionals between 9 AM and 5 PM PT to review your information. |

### Share good news when it happens

Let customers know when things are complete or they reach a goal.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| You earned $2,500 more this month than October 2021! [See more reports] | You made a deposit! It might take some time to process. |
| The IRS accepted your tax return! [Track your refund] | You've downloaded the GoPayment App! But why haven't you processed a payment yet? |

For more about celebrating good news, [check out the celebrations pattern](https://contentdesign.intuit.com/content-patterns/celebrations/).

### Share bad news directly

When things fail, clearly state what happened and what we're doing to fix it—or what the customer can do to make it right.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| The IRS is down and returns cannot be processed right now. Visit the IRS website to stay updated. [Check status] | We're unable to process your return at this time for reasons outside our control. |

For more about delivering bad news, [check out the bad news content pattern](https://contentdesign.intuit.com/content-patterns/bad-news/).

## In-product notifications best practices

An in-product notification is served to a customer when they are already engaged with an Intuit product. If these notifications aren't useful, customers will ignore them.

### Context is just as important as content

Notify customers in a context related to the notification. Keep content focused on helping them discover new features they can benefit from at the moment.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Did you move this year? Let's make sure your W-2 is sent to the right address. [Review info] | Your W-2 will be sent from your employer sometime between 1/1 and 2/1. In order to make sure you can do your taxes on time, it's important to make sure your address is correct. |
| You earned $2,400 more this month than in October 2020. Get a closer look at your business growth. | We've got news about your business growth! |
| Tax Profile Status: Incomplete. We're missing a few key details. Complete your profile so it's 100% ready for TurboTax. | Bad news: Your tax profile is incomplete. |
| Send an invoice to get payments deposited in your checking account. [Receive your first payment] | You won't get paid until you start sending invoices. Ready? |
| You can now schedule or pay your bills directly from your QuickBooks Checking account. [Pay a bill] | Check out our new and improved QuickBooks Checking features! |

### Personalize to delight

Use what we know about customers to create moments of delight and comfort. Speak directly to the customer's needs—we're here to help them, not get in the way.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Was your tax refund accurate? Update your tax withholding to meet your financial goals. [View tax withholding] | Review your tax withholding information. Make sure your tax withholding is up-to-date so you get the correct refund amount. |
| Your tax return is ready! Take a look so Jorge can file before the free offer expires. | You might miss your tax deadline! Jorge must review your return or you can't file with this offer. |
| Take photos of your receipts with your phone, and we'll keep them safe until tax season. | Don't forget to save your receipts for tax time. |
| Check {bank account name} (...1234) and verify 2 small deposits to start moving money. [Verify your bank] | Verify your bank now to start making money moves. |

## Push notifications best practices

Push notifications are messages that an app sends to a customer's device. They can engage people even when they're not using the product. People need to have notifications turned on to receive them.

Keep in mind guidelines specific to [iOS](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/ios/system-capabilities/notifications) and [Android](https://developer.android.com/guide/topics/ui/notifiers/notifications) notifications.

### Limit frequency

As a rule, use fewer notifications. People get lots of notifications from the apps they use—more will make people annoyed and they'll turn them off.

Before sending a push notification, ask yourself:

- How can we bundle notifications?
- How often should they receive this type of notification?
- How many other notifications are they receiving from us?
- When was the last time they received a notification from us?
- Does this need to be a push notification? Is it better suited to another type?

### Use emoji with care

Use emoji in push notifications to add feeling, but not too many. Remember culture, accessibility, and tone when you choose them. [Check out our emoji guidelines](https://contentdesign.intuit.com/style/emoji/).

### Avoid sensitive information

Push notifications can be seen by anyone, so don't include private information that could hurt customers if someone saw it.

**Examples**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Sign in to TurboTax from Santa Clara, CA | Yikes! Looks like someone is trying to access TurboTax with your device from Santa Clara. |
| Your April money stats are in! Let's see how it went. | You were $3,000 over budget in April. Better luck next month. |
| Did you just make a big purchase? Let's confirm this transaction. | Did you spend $1,078? Confirm now. |
