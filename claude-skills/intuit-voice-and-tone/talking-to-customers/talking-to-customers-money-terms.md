# Money terms

**Source:** https://contentdesign.intuit.com/talking-to-customers/money-terms/

---

As much as we'd like to, we can't just stop using the language of taxes, accounting, finance, and technology. We have two approaches to help our customers understand these terms.

Customers will need to talk to accountants, lenders, and other finance and tax people, and those people are bound to use terms like deductions, liability, profit and loss, and so on. As we introduce and define these terms, we want to empower customers with knowledge, not make them feel defeated by speaking a different language.

---

## Two approaches to defining financial terms

### Approach 1: State the term first, then define it in simple words

This approach works best for tax terms, as customers may have already heard these words, such as deductions and earned-income tax credit.

### Approach 2: Describe the term in simple words first, then give it a name

After describing it simply, use the term and maybe include a reminder. This approach works best for accounting terms like general ledger or chart of accounts.

---

## Examples

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Here's a list of your tax categories. Accountants call this a chart of accounts. | Here's your Chart of Accounts. |
| Do you own this business? Or as lawyers like to say, "Are you the responsible party here?" | Are you the responsible party for this business? |
| Double-check the tax category and add it to your books. | Reconcile this transaction. |
| We'll import all the transactions for you. | We'll get all the transactions from your bank. We call this "importing." |
| Have customers transfer money directly to your account. | Get paid via ACH. |
