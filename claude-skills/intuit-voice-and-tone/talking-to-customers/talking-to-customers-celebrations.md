# Celebrations

**Source:** https://contentdesign.intuit.com/talking-to-customers/celebrations/

---

Celebrations help dramatize the benefit and create emotive experiences for our customers. They drive a sense of accomplishment and encourage folks as they work toward their goals.

---

## When we celebrate

Celebrations are an opportunity to connect with our customers and amplify their accomplishments. To retain their impact, they should be used sparingly. If we celebrate too often, and for minor accomplishments, celebrations could become annoying, or worse, condescending ("Hurray! You made a credit card payment!"). So choose these moments mindfully.

**A useful rule of thumb:** Celebrate when it's something the customer accomplished, not something we did or that benefits us.

**Examples of customer accomplishments worth celebrating:**
- The customer receives a promised payoff from our product (first completed payroll run, first payment received and deposited in their account, an expert signed off on their taxes)
- The customer sees value from changing a key habit (more savings, less debt, books closed on time)
- The customer achieved an outcome that's important to them (finished their taxes, their refund was deposited, they have a high credit score)
- The customer achieved a major milestone (anniversary of starting their business, completed their taxes, reached a sales milestone, secured a loan)
- The customer reached a goal they set (paying off a credit card or reaching a savings goal)

**Other points to consider:**
- Is there any way your well-intended celebration could backfire? For example, a tax break might seem awesome, but not if it's due to medical expenses for a terminally ill loved one. Double-check your blind spots and biases to make sure our content doesn’t cause harm.
- Would a celebration undermine customer confidence in our products? For example, does celebrating a successful data import imply that it's usually unsuccessful?
- What other celebrations might be happening nearby in the customer experience?
- How often does the moment you want to celebrate occur? If it's daily, celebration might not be the best approach.

Think about celebrations as being on the far end of the spectrum of how we acknowledge customer accomplishments. Question whether you need a celebration when a simple success message might be more appropriate.

## Celebration vs. success message

| Celebration | Success message |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Ran first (or 100th) payroll | Set a spending target |
| Reached savings goal for vacation | Created a budget |
| Paid off a loan or credit card | Completed a basic workflow or recurring task |
| Loan approved through QuickBooks Capital | Turned on a preference |
| Payment received (show actual amount) | Made progress toward a goal |
| Potential tax deduction (show actual amount) | Categorized a trip that's not a deduction |
| | Submitted loan request |
| | Sent an invoice |
| | Turned on mileage tracking |

## How to celebrate

### Celebrate to scale

Celebrations come in many sizes. Consider creating a framework for your celebration strategy that maps customer experiences to different levels of recognition.

**Example framework from QuickBooks:**

#### Level 1 — Small moments
Recognize the work the customer did or what QuickBooks did for them. Can occur more often. The celebration motivates customers to keep making progress toward bigger goals and encourages them to turn a good behavior into a habit.

> *You made your quarterly tax payment. Nice!*

#### Level 2 — Medium moments
When a customer finds value in a key habit or hits certain milestones. The celebration encourages good behavior that's on the right track.

> *You hit $100 in potential deductions.*

#### Level 3 — Large moments
Occur when a customer has achieved a major goal using the product. They can occur at an earlier point in the journey (for example, a QuickBooks Live match) or toward the end of a journey (such as an export for tax time). We dial up the visuals and motion to communicate these rare and unique moments.

> *Manuela, your federal tax return was accepted!*

## Leave them speechless

You can incorporate moments of delight without using a lot of words. Sometimes you don't need words at all. The right visuals can convey a congrats message without actually saying congratulations: think delightful icons and motion, but go easy on the confetti.

Don't forget to include the celebratory vibe when making your visuals accessible with alt text. Partner with your visual designer to choose colors, shapes, illustrations, and motion that enhance your celebration moments.
