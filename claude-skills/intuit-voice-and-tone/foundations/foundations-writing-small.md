# Writing small

**Source:** https://contentdesign.intuit.com/foundations/writing-small/

---

No matter what type of content you're writing at Intuit, less is (almost always) more. Customers come to our products to accomplish tasks, not to get bogged down in words.

Whether a help article or a chatbot conversation, customers scan to find what's important to them. They want to consume info that's accurate, concise, clear, and conversational.

---

## Keep it short

Look at your content, word by word, and challenge yourself to:

- **Guide with structure.** Help customers find your message by using hierarchy, bullet points, and visual space.
- **Focus on what's useful.** Customers read only what's important to them.
- **Keep it simple.** A complicated page makes the product look complicated and intimidating. Simple equals easy.
- **Use less content.** Fewer words means a lighter cognitive load.
- **Make it scannable.** Most people have limited attention and skim the content.

Make sure every word has a clear purpose and provides value to the customer. If it doesn't—cut it. Be brutal! Only add back words if you start losing the meaning.

### Take a little off the top

Headlines should be tight and specific. They should quickly convey the gist of the message and let the reader decide whether they want to read more or skip ahead.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Get expert help with Full Service | You told us you wanted to hand your taxes off to a tax expert. Ready to continue? |

## Prioritize what customers need

According to [Nielsen Norman Group](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/how-people-read-online/), people rarely read online—they scan. It's important to front-load key messages. If the content is critical, put it in front, on top, or in an eye-catching location.

Use headlines and even CTAs to flag important information. For modals—which users often dismiss quickly—the CTA might be the element doing most of the informational work, so make it clear and value-packed.

**Example:** The key message is "We need your W-2 to move forward."

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| **Let's get your W-2** — To get started, we'll need a digital copy of your form—a PDF, JPG, or PNG. | OK, if you have a pdf copy of your W-2, we can use it to save time. When you upload it, we'll pull the info directly over. Don't have a PDF? That's OK. We'll help you type your info. |

## Use progressive disclosure

Progressive disclosure lets the customer focus on what's in front of them before moving on to the next task. This is especially helpful for long forms or workflows, where the customer can quickly become overwhelmed.

**Benefits of progressive disclosure:**

- Provides a feeling of making progress
- Saves time
- Lowers cognitive load
- Prevents overwhelm
- Helps prioritize customers' attention

Progressive disclosure is also a foundation of inclusive design. Some neurodiverse people (like those with ADHD or anxiety) might find a wall of text to be too much and abandon the task.

**Ways to progressively disclose info:**

- **Step-throughs** — Break a process into bite-sized pieces. The customer answers questions clustered into logical groups and only progresses to the next section when they've completed the current one.
- **Conditional fields** — Ask the customer to fill in one option (e.g., text or email), then display follow-up fields based on their selection.
- **Accordion components** — Can be collapsed or expanded to see relevant information.
- **Links** — Take the customer to a web page with more details about an offer or process.

## Break it up

No one opens fintech software hoping to read lengthy narratives. Content hierarchy lets you structure content to help the reader quickly find details. Try using bullets to break big chunks of text into smaller, digestible pieces.

**Example**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Support includes the cost of: Transportation and similar necessities, Recreation, Medical and dental care, Education, Clothing, Lodging, Food | Generally, support is what you spend on a person to provide the basic life necessities. Whether you're supporting a friend or a relative, the term "support" is generally defined as what you spend on a person to provide the basic necessities of life... |

Bullets aren't the only way to help readers browse. Images and buttons can also be used to help tell the story and move a customer along.

## Tips for writing small

First, decide what to say. Then find the shortest way to say it.

### Worth the space vs. not worth the space

| Worth the space | Not worth the space |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Explaining the payoff:** Subscribe and save 50%. / Update your billing info so you don't lose access. / At tax time, you'll be ready. / Track your mileage automatically and get 57.5¢ a mile. | **Detailed info about the future:** Next January, tap Taxes, then Annual taxes to see your 2020 tax checklist. When we're done with this step, we'll move on to the final phase... |
| **Reducing work and worry:** Select all that applied in 2020. / Your best guess is fine. / You can change this anytime. / No employees? You can skip this part. | **Educational content they didn't ask for:** Ready to see your chart of accounts? Before you continue, you should know a bit about how the chart of accounts works... |
| **Guiding them to the right answer:** What kind of work do you do? (Example: construction) / Based on your info, X is right for you. / Construction businesses usually pick X. | **Exhaustive lists:** With QuickBooks invoicing, you can customize invoices to showcase your brand, add your logo, preview what the invoice looks like, accept online payments, get notified when customers view and pay, convert estimates to invoices, and save invoices as a draft. |
| **Preventing mistakes:** If you edit X, it will change Y. / After you send this, you won't be able to change it. / Ask your accountant before making changes. | |

## Tips to cut characters

### Follow our style guide

- Use ampersands in menus and labels to join related items. Avoid them in headlines and body content.
- Use common abbreviations and acronyms.
- Use common contractions.
- Use active voice and simple tenses.

### Design on mobile screens first

- Design for common devices (like the iPhone) but make a plan for all devices and languages.
- Leave space for text to wrap or have it dynamically change to a smaller font size.
- Ask for mobile mocks or make your own.

### Ditch filler words

Remove these words wherever possible:

- for the most part
- even
- simply
- just
- basically
- really
- actually
- very
- slightly
- only
- that
- a little
- kind of
- sort of
- is designed to

### Skip unneeded prepositions

| Wordy | Tight |
|-------|-------|
| Meet up with a client | Meet a client |
| Inventory you sold off | Inventory you sold |
| Where did it go to? | Where did it go? |

### Use short words and phrases

| Wordy | Tight |
|-------|-------|
| in the event of | if |
| overly | too |
| in addition | plus, also |
| which means | so |
| when the time comes | later |
| generally speaking, in most cases, for a lot of people | usually |
| in some cases, there are times when | sometimes |
| often times | often |
| a small number of | a few |
| quite a while | a while |
| a little bit, a small amount of | a bit, some |

### Start with a verb

| Wordy | Tight |
|-------|-------|
| This is where you can find X | Find X |
| You can do it all in one place | Do it all in one place |

### Use hard-working verbs

| Wordy | Tight |
|-------|-------|
| Enter your details to schedule an appointment with a TurboTax expert in under 5 minutes | Connect with an expert in under 5 minutes |
| You'll receive a charge for | You pay |
| Use your camera to capture | Snap a photo |
| How much debt you have | What you owe |
| Record that the invoice got paid | Mark the invoice paid |

### Make it a question

| Wordy | Tight |
|-------|-------|
| Let's find out where your refund is | Where's that refund? |
| Enter the due date | When is it due? |
| Enter your payment method | How did you pay? |

### Start your question another way

| Wordy | Tight |
|-------|-------|
| Which customer paid you? | Who paid you? |
| What region are you in? | Where are you? |

### Try removing the who or what

| Wordy | Tight |
|-------|-------|
| We can help you | We can help |
| Here's why you should do it | Here's why |
| Need help? Reach out to us. | Need help? Reach out. |
| See how much money you spent | See how much you spent |
| Taxes are due April 15. Let's get ready to file. | Taxes are due April 15. Let's get ready |
| Forgot your password? Let’s reset your sign-in info. | Forgot your password? Let’s reset it. |
| How did you pay for it? | How did you pay? |
| Snap a photo of your 1099-INT form | Snap a photo |
| [Name], unlock more savings on credit cards, loans, investments, and more | [Name], unlock more savings |
| Scan the barcode on the back of your driver's license | Scan the barcode on the back |

### Use sentence fragments

| Wordy | Tight |
|-------|-------|
| Why do you need to know my marital status? | Why we're asking |
| Are you done editing? | Done editing? |
| Here's how it works | How it works |
| We hope to see you soon | See you soon! |
| Do you want to save your changes? | Save your changes |
| Your changes have been saved | Changes saved |

### Don't be robotic

| Robotic | Human |
|---------|-------|
| Action failed | That didn't work |
| Location settings incorrect. Fix now. | Mileage tracking is blocked. Let's fix it. |

### Don't leave out crucial info

| Too short | Clear |
|-----------|-------|
| Refresh | Pull down to refresh |
| Add your loan info | Add your loan info (not student loans) |
| Enter total | Enter total before tax |

## Context matters

In headlines, subheaders, and body text, use possessives (your, their) and articles (a, the).

In buttons, labels, menus, and confirmations, leave out possessives and articles. There isn't always space, and we want to be consistent.

**Exception:** In onboarding, feel free to use possessives in buttons (example: "Get my discount") and more conversational labels and confirmations.
