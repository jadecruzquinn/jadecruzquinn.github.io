## AP Stylebook and dictionaries

AP Stylebook is our default for many style, punctuation, and grammar things. If there are Intuit-specific terms or differences from the AP guidance (like e-book vs ebook, for example), then we document these changes on [contentdesign.intuit.com](https://contentdesign.intuit.com/).

Intuit employees: You can access AP Stylebook anytime, anywhere when you're on VPN. [Go to AP Stylebook](http://apstylebook.com/intuit-2/ap_stylebook)

For specific words not noted on this site, check Merriam-Webster (US), the Canadian Oxford Dictionary (CA), or the Collins English Dictionary (UK and RoW).

## NotebookLM and generative AI

Using the tool [Notebook LM](https://notebooklm.google.com/), you can add the content design site (along with any project or team-specific resources) as a reference to ground the model's knowledge.

For more on how to do this, as well as other generative AI use cases, go to [Using AI](https://contentdesign.intuit.com/ai/using-ai/) (Intuit employees only).

## Writer

This language-checking tool uses AI and our custom style guide to help content designers create consistent, quality content. It's like a copyeditor on your shoulder.

To find out about installing the Chrome extension, join the #cmty-writer-ai Slack channel.

## Design tools

Content designers across Intuit use a range of design tools. Check with your team to make sure you're using the tools that are best for your type of work.

Your team might be using one or more of these:

- **Google Drive** — file storage and sharing
- **Mural or Figjam** — virtual whiteboarding
- **Adobe Creative Cloud** — Photoshop, Spark, and other apps
- **Figma** — cloud-based product design
